## Company name
Zion Garden (Papy Peter Permaculture)

## Offering
Zion Garden designs and delivers practical permaculture programs in Wallonia and Brussels. Our core services include: (1) school garden programs for active learning, (2) therapeutic gardens for nursing homes, psychiatric institutions, and social shelters, and (3) corporate workshops and conferences focused on wellbeing, CSR, and team cohesion. We provide hands-on implementation, training, and follow-up so projects are concrete, useful, and sustainable.

## Customer profile
Best-fit customers are schools, care institutions, nonprofits, and companies located in Mons (priority), Wallonia, and Brussels. Ideal organizations typically have 10–500 staff and are looking for educational innovation, resident/patient wellbeing activities, or practical CSR initiatives. Key buyer/user titles: School Director, Educational Coordinator, Head of Care, Occupational Therapist, Facility Manager, HR Manager, CSR Manager, and Committee/Works Council representatives. Strong fit signals include interest in wellbeing programs, pedagogical innovation, therapeutic activities, green transition, and community impact.

## Pain points
Before working with us, customers often struggle with low engagement in educational/care activities, lack of practical wellbeing initiatives, and difficulty turning CSR intentions into visible action. Many teams lack internal expertise, time, and structure to build and maintain a meaningful garden project. As a result, opportunities are lost: reduced learner motivation, limited therapeutic stimulation, weak team cohesion, and underused outdoor spaces.

## Value proposition
Zion Garden turns underused spaces into practical, high-impact garden programs that improve engagement, wellbeing, and learning outcomes. Clients get a clear action plan, on-site execution, and training that teams can continue independently. Schools gain active learning environments, care institutions gain therapeutic activity frameworks, and companies gain tangible CSR and team-building outcomes. The result is faster implementation, stronger participation, and visible social/environmental value.

## Advantage over competitors
Our differentiation is execution on the ground combined with pedagogy and therapeutic application. We do not stop at advice: we design, implement, train, and follow up. Zion Garden uniquely combines school permaculture, therapeutic garden activities, and corporate awareness formats under one approach. We also integrate medicinal plant gardens and practical inspiration from Japanese and Chinese philosophies, adapted to local realities. Clients choose us for practical results, human delivery, and local proximity.

## Primary competitors
Direct and indirect alternatives include local landscaping companies, environmental education nonprofits, independent permaculture trainers, therapeutic activity providers, and internal DIY initiatives managed by staff. Prospects may also compare us with generic wellbeing workshops, CSR consulting agencies, or institutions that run occasional garden projects without full implementation support.
